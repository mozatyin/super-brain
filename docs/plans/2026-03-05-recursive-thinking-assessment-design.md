# Recursive Thinking Assessment / 递归思维能力评估

## Purpose / 目的

Assess candidates' capacity for **exploratory problem-solving** — the ability to work with AI through iterative cycles of hypothesis, testing, and refinement (PDCA). This assessment identifies people who can navigate ambiguity, question assumptions, make non-obvious connections, and collaborate with AI as a thinking partner.

评估候选人的**探索性问题解决能力** — 能够与AI通过假设-测试-调整的迭代循环（PDCA）协作的能力。本评估识别能够在模糊中前行、质疑假设、发现非显而易见的联系、并将AI作为思维伙伴的人。

## Two Use Cases / 两个使用场景

| | Internal Screen / 内部筛选 | External Hire / 外部招聘 |
|---|---|---|
| **Duration** | 12-15 min | 30 min |
| **Format** | 12 MC questions | 12 MC + 2 open-ended scenarios |
| **Purpose** | Select for PDCA AI Agent core team | Evaluate candidates in interviews |
| **Scoring** | Automated (0-36) | MC automated + open-ended human scoring (0-66) |

## The 5 Cognitive Dimensions / 五个认知维度

### 1. Pattern Transfer / 跨域迁移 (3 questions)
**What it measures**: Ability to see structural similarities across unrelated domains and apply solutions from one context to another.

**测量内容**: 能够跨越不相关领域发现结构性相似性，将一个情境的解决方案应用到另一个情境。

- **Low / 低**: Solves problems only within familiar domain. Doesn't see analogies.
- **High / 高**: Rapidly connects ideas from unrelated fields. "This is structurally the same as..."

### 2. Assumption Questioning / 假设质疑 (2 questions)
**What it measures**: Tendency to challenge hidden assumptions in problem framing before diving into solutions.

**测量内容**: 在投入解决方案之前质疑问题框架中隐藏假设的倾向。

- **Low / 低**: Accepts problem framing at face value. Follows instructions literally.
- **High / 高**: Immediately asks "why are we assuming X?" Reframes the problem.

### 3. Iterative Structuring / 迭代建构 (3 questions)
**What it measures**: Comfort with ambiguity and ability to extract structure through rapid cycles of hypothesis-test-adjust.

**测量内容**: 对模糊性的适应能力，以及通过快速的假设-测试-调整循环从混沌中提炼结构的能力。

- **Low / 低**: Wants complete information before starting. Paralyzed by ambiguity.
- **High / 高**: Dives in with a rough hypothesis, tests it quickly, adjusts. "Let me try this and see."

### 4. Meta-cognition / 元认知 (2 questions)
**What it measures**: Awareness of one's own thinking process — knowing when you're stuck, what you don't know, and when to pivot.

**测量内容**: 对自身思维过程的觉察 — 知道何时卡住、不知道什么、以及何时需要转换策略。

- **Low / 低**: Doesn't notice when going in circles. Keeps pushing the same failing approach.
- **High / 高**: Recognizes "I'm going in circles" and changes strategy. Knows what they don't know.

### 5. AI Collaboration Mindset / AI协作思维 (2 questions)
**What it measures**: Ability to frame problems for AI iteratively, use AI output to refine the question, and recognize AI limitations.

**测量内容**: 能够迭代地为AI构建问题、利用AI输出来精炼问题、并识别AI局限性的能力。

- **Low / 低**: Treats AI as a search engine. One-shot queries. Accepts first output.
- **High / 高**: Iterates with AI as a thinking partner. Refines prompts based on output. Knows when AI is wrong.

## Part 1: Multiple Choice Questions / 选择题 (12 questions)

**Scoring / 评分**: Each question 0-3 points. Options are NOT labeled with point values during administration.

**Design principle / 设计原则**: No "obviously right" answer. All options sound reasonable. The difference is in the depth of thinking they reveal. 没有"明显正确"的答案。所有选项都听起来合理。差别在于思维深度。

### Q1 — Pattern Transfer / 跨域迁移

**EN**: Your team is struggling with user onboarding — 70% of new users drop off in the first week. You're asked to fix it. What's your first move?

**CN**: 你的团队在用户引导方面遇到困难——70%的新用户在第一周流失。你被要求解决这个问题。你的第一步是什么？

| Option | EN | CN | Pts |
|--------|----|----|-----|
| A | Analyze the onboarding funnel data to find where users drop off | 分析引导漏斗数据，找出用户在哪里流失 | 0 |
| B | Interview 10 users who dropped off to understand their experience | 访谈10位流失用户了解他们的体验 | 1 |
| C | Study how a completely different product (like a mobile game) keeps users engaged in their first hour, then apply those principles | 研究一个完全不同的产品（如手游）如何在第一小时留住用户，然后应用这些原则 | 2 |
| D | Question whether "onboarding" is the right frame — maybe the real problem is time-to-value: the product doesn't deliver meaningful value fast enough | 质疑"引导"是否是正确的框架——也许真正的问题是价值交付时间：产品没有足够快地提供有意义的价值 | 3 |

**Rationale**: A is data-first but stays in frame. B adds empathy. C shows cross-domain transfer. D reframes the assumption entirely.

**EN**: Your manager says: "We need to hire 5 more engineers because the team is shipping too slowly." What's your response?

**CN**: 你的经理说："我们需要再招5个工程师，因为团队交付太慢了。"你的回应是什么？

| Option | EN | CN | Pts |
|--------|----|----|-----|

### Q5 — Assumption Questioning / 假设质疑

**EN**: Your product has a 4.2-star rating on the app store with 10,000 reviews. The product team wants to improve it to 4.5 stars. They propose fixing the top 5 complaints from 1-star reviews. What's your take?

**CN**: 你的产品在应用商店上有10,000条评论，4.2星评分。产品团队希望提高到4.5星，提议修复1星评论中排名前5的投诉。你怎么看？

| Option | EN | CN | Pts |
|--------|----|----|-----|
| A | Makes sense — the 1-star reviews are the biggest detractors, fixing them should improve the average | 有道理——1星评论是最大的拖累因素，修复它们应该能提高平均分 | 0 |
| B | Also look at 3-star reviews — they're the "swing voters" who could go either way with small improvements | 也要看3星评论——他们是"摇摆选民"，小改进就可能让他们转向 | 1 |
| C | Analyze whether the 1-star reviewers are even in the target user segment — if they're not, fixing their issues might not help the rating and could harm the experience for core users | 分析1星评论者是否属于目标用户群——如果不是，修复他们的问题可能不会提高评分，反而可能损害核心用户的体验 | 2 |
| D | Question the metric: 4.2→4.5 is cosmetic. The real question is whether happy users are growing the product through word-of-mouth. A 4.0-star app that grows 20% monthly is better than a 4.5-star app that's flat | 质疑指标本身：4.2→4.5是表面的。真正的问题是开心的用户是否通过口碑增长产品。一个月增长20%的4.0星应用比一个持平的4.5星应用好 | 3 |

**Rationale**: A accepts everything. B stays in frame but optimizes. C questions the user segment assumption. D questions the entire goal — rating vs. growth.

### Q6 — Iterative Structuring / 迭代建构

**EN**: You're given a new project with no clear requirements: "Build something that helps our sales team be more effective." There's no spec, no deadline, and no one seems to know exactly what "more effective" means. What do you do first?

**CN**: 你接到一个没有明确需求的新项目："做一个让销售团队更高效的东西。"没有规格说明、没有截止日期，也没有人确切知道"更高效"意味着什么。你首先做什么？

| Option | EN | CN | Pts |
|--------|----|----|-----|
| A | Ask stakeholders to define requirements and success metrics before starting any work | 要求利益相关者在开始任何工作之前定义需求和成功指标 | 0 |
| B | Shadow the sales team for a week to observe their workflow and identify pain points | 跟踪销售团队一周，观察他们的工作流程并识别痛点 | 1 |
| C | Build a rough prototype in 2 days that solves the ONE most obvious pain point, put it in front of the team, and iterate based on their reaction | 在2天内构建一个粗略原型，解决最明显的一个痛点，放在团队面前，根据他们的反应迭代 | 2 |
| D | Talk to 3 top performers and 3 struggling performers for 30 min each, identify the ONE behavioral difference, build a quick test to see if that difference can be taught/automated, then iterate | 分别与3个顶级销售和3个困难销售交谈30分钟，找出一个关键的行为差异，快速测试这个差异是否可以教授/自动化，然后迭代 | 3 |

**Rationale**: A stalls waiting for clarity. B is observational but passive. C is iterative but picks the target randomly. D is iterative AND strategic — finds the highest-leverage insight first, then iterates on that specific insight.

### Q7 — Iterative Structuring / 迭代建构

**EN**: You're 3 weeks into a project. Your initial approach seemed promising, but new data shows it's unlikely to work. You've already invested significant effort. What do you do?

**CN**: 项目进行了3周。你最初的方案看起来很有希望，但新数据表明它可能行不通。你已经投入了大量精力。你会怎么做？

| Option | EN | CN | Pts |
|--------|----|----|-----|
| A | Push through — 3 weeks of work shouldn't be thrown away. Adjust the approach slightly and see if it works | 坚持下去——3周的工作不应该被丢弃。稍微调整方案看是否可行 | 0 |
| B | Present the data to the team and ask for a group decision on whether to pivot | 将数据展示给团队，让大家共同决定是否转向 | 1 |
| C | Stop, extract what you learned from the failed approach (what DID work, what constraints did you discover), then use those lessons to design Approach v2 | 停下来，从失败的方案中提取学到的东西（什么有效、发现了什么约束），然后用这些经验设计方案v2 | 2 |
| D | Ask: "What would I do if I were starting today with everything I now know?" If the answer is fundamentally different, pivot immediately. The 3 weeks aren't wasted — they bought you knowledge | 问自己："如果我今天带着所有已知信息重新开始，我会怎么做？"如果答案根本不同，立即转向。3周没有浪费——它们买到了知识 | 3 |

**Rationale**: A is sunk-cost fallacy. B deflects. C is good — extracts learnings before pivoting. D is the highest-level response — reframes the sunk cost as purchased knowledge, applies a clean "fresh start" test.

### Q8 — Iterative Structuring / 迭代建构

**EN**: You're debugging a production issue. You have three conflicting theories from three different team members about the root cause. Each theory has some supporting evidence. What's your approach?

**CN**: 你正在调试一个生产环境问题。三个不同的团队成员提出了三个相互矛盾的根因理论。每个理论都有一些支持证据。你的做法是什么？

| Option | EN | CN | Pts |
|--------|----|----|-----|
| A | Go with the theory from the most experienced team member | 采用最有经验的团队成员的理论 | 0 |
| B | Design a test that could distinguish between the three theories, then run it | 设计一个能区分三个理论的测试，然后执行 | 1 |
| C | Find the simplest experiment that could DISPROVE each theory. Run the fastest disproval test first to narrow the space quickly | 找到能否定每个理论的最简单实验。先运行最快的否定测试以快速缩小范围 | 2 |
| D | Step back: if all three theories have evidence, maybe they're all partially right. Ask "what would the root cause look like if all three observations are true simultaneously?" That might reveal a deeper cause | 退后一步：如果三个理论都有证据，也许它们都部分正确。问"如果三个观察同时为真，根因会是什么样的？"这可能揭示更深层的原因 | 3 |

**Rationale**: A is authority-based. B is scientific but inefficient. C is efficient falsification (Popperian). D is the deepest — uses the contradictions as data, looks for a unifying explanation.

### Q9 — Meta-cognition / 元认知

**EN**: You've been working on a problem for 2 hours and feel stuck. You've tried 4 different approaches and none have worked. What do you do?

**CN**: 你在一个问题上工作了2小时，感觉卡住了。你尝试了4种不同的方法，都没有成功。你会怎么做？

| Option | EN | CN | Pts |
|--------|----|----|-----|
| A | Take a break, come back with fresh eyes | 休息一下，带着新的视角回来 | 0 |
| B | Ask a colleague for help — a fresh perspective might see what you're missing | 找同事帮忙——新的视角可能看到你忽略的东西 | 1 |
| C | Stop trying to solve it and instead write down exactly WHY each of the 4 approaches failed. The pattern of failures might reveal the actual constraint you haven't identified | 停止尝试解决，而是写下每种方法失败的确切原因。失败的模式可能揭示你尚未识别的实际约束 | 2 |
| D | Ask yourself: "Am I solving the right problem?" List all the assumptions you've been making. The fact that 4 approaches failed suggests you might be operating on a wrong assumption about the problem itself | 问自己："我在解决正确的问题吗？"列出你一直在做的所有假设。4种方法都失败的事实表明你可能对问题本身有错误的假设 | 3 |

**Rationale**: A resets but doesn't learn. B is social but passive. C is metacognitive — analyzing failure patterns. D is the deepest metacognition — questioning whether the problem framing is wrong.

**EN**: You're preparing a presentation for the board. You've done thorough research and feel confident in your recommendation. A junior team member raises a concern you hadn't considered. It's a good point but incorporating it would require rethinking your entire approach with only 2 days until the presentation. What do you do?

**CN**: 你正在为董事会准备演示。你做了充分的研究，对自己的建议很有信心。一个初级团队成员提出了一个你没考虑过的担忧。这是个好观点，但纳入它需要重新思考整个方案，而距离演示只有2天。你会怎么做？

| Option | EN | CN | Pts |
|--------|----|----|-----|
| A | Note the concern but proceed with the current plan — there's not enough time to rethink everything | 记下这个担忧但继续当前计划——没有足够时间重新思考一切 | 0 |
| B | Add the concern as a "risk" slide and present both the recommendation and the potential issue | 将担忧添加为"风险"幻灯片，同时展示建议和潜在问题 | 1 |
| C | Spend 2 hours pressure-testing whether the concern actually invalidates the recommendation. If it does, rethink the approach even if it means a rougher presentation | 花2小时压力测试这个担忧是否真的否定了建议。如果是，即使演示会粗糙一些也要重新思考方案 | 2 |

Being wrong in a polished way is worse than being right in a rough way | 感谢他们并问："如果这个担忧成立，正确的建议应该是什么？"如果他们的回答揭示了一条明显更好的路径，立即切换。精致地犯错比粗糙地正确更糟 | 3 |

**Rationale**: A is time-pressure bias. B is diplomatic but avoidant. C is integrity-driven but still own-perspective-limited. D is the best meta-cognition: uses the junior person's insight as a thinking partner, immediately tests the alternative, and has the clarity that "polished wrong < rough right."

**CN**: 你让AI撰写一份策略文档。输出写得很好，但感觉哪里不对——你无法确切指出问题所在。你会怎么做？

## Part 2: Open-Ended Scenarios / 开放题 (External only)

### Scenario 1: The Stalled Initiative / 停滞的项目
*Tests: Meta-cognition + Iterative Structuring + Assumption Questioning*

**EN**:

You're leading a project to reduce customer support tickets by 50% in 6 months. After 3 months:
- Rebuilt FAQ page → tickets down 5%
- Added AI chatbot → tickets down 8%
- Improved error messages → tickets down 3%
- Total: only 16% reduction (target: 50%)

The team is demoralized. The CEO is asking for a plan. You have 3 months left.

Additional context: The chatbot handles 200 tickets/day but creates 50 new "chatbot complaint" tickets. The FAQ gets 10,000 views/day but users still submit tickets about FAQ-covered topics. One engineer mentioned that 40% of all tickets come from one feature that was redesigned 4 months ago.

**Questions**:
1. Before making a plan, what's the FIRST thing you'd investigate and why? (5 pts)
2. What hidden assumption in this project might be wrong? (5 pts)
3. Describe a fundamentally different approach. Where did the inspiration come from? (5 pts)

**CN**:

你负责一个项目，目标是6个月内将客服工单减少50%。3个月后：
- 重建FAQ页面 → 工单减少5%
- 添加AI聊天机器人 → 工单减少8%
- 改善错误信息 → 工单减少3%
- 总计：仅减少16%（目标：50%）

团队士气低落。CEO要求一个计划。你还有3个月。

额外信息：聊天机器人每天处理200张工单，但产生了50张新的"聊天机器人投诉"工单。FAQ每天有10,000次浏览，但用户仍然提交关于FAQ已覆盖主题的工单。一个工程师提到，40%的工单来自4个月前重新设计的一个功能。

**问题**：
1. 在制定计划之前，你首先会调查什么？为什么？（5分）
2. 这个项目中隐藏的什么假设可能是错误的？（5分）
3. 描述一种根本不同的方法。灵感来自哪里？（5分）

**Scoring rubric / 评分标准**:

| Score | Q1: First Investigation | Q2: Hidden Assumption | Q3: Different Approach |
|-------|------------------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| 0-1 | "Look at the data" (vague) | Can't identify one | Same type of solution (another tool, better chatbot) |
| 2-3 | Notices the 40% one-feature clue or chatbot creating tickets | "We assumed reducing = deflecting, but the chatbot is creating new problems" | A different approach from a related domain |
| 4-5 | Immediately goes to the 40% clue AND explains why: "If one feature causes 40% of tickets, that's 200+/day — fixing THAT feature directly is more impactful than all three initiatives combined" | Identifies the deepest assumption: "We assumed tickets are a symptom to be deflected. They might be a signal — the product is telling us what's broken. The real project is 'fix the product' not 'reduce tickets'" | A fundamentally different frame from an unrelated domain (e.g., "in medicine, you treat the disease, not the symptoms" or "Toyota's andon cord — stop the line, fix the cause") |

### Scenario 2: The AI Collaboration / AI协作场景
*Tests: AI Collaboration + Pattern Transfer + Iterative Structuring*

**EN**:

You're tasked with creating a new employee training program for your company (200 people, mix of engineering and non-engineering). You decide to use AI to help design it. Here's your first interaction:

> **You**: Design a training program for my company.
>
> **AI**: Here's a comprehensive 12-week training program:
> - Week 1-2: Onboarding fundamentals
> - Week 3-4: Role-specific skills
> - Week 5-8: Cross-functional projects
> - Week 9-10: Leadership development
> - Week 11-12: Assessment and feedback
> Each module includes lectures, hands-on exercises, and peer reviews...

The AI output is well-structured and sounds professional.

**Questions**:
1. What's wrong with this AI interaction? How would you redo your prompt? (5 pts)
2. After getting a better response, what would your SECOND prompt to the AI be? (5 pts)
3. How would you know when to STOP iterating with AI and start doing something in the real world? (5 pts)

**CN**:

你被要求为公司（200人，工程和非工程混合）创建一个新的员工培训计划。你决定使用AI来帮助设计。以下是你的第一次交互：

> **你**：为我的公司设计一个培训计划。
>
> **AI**：这是一个全面的12周培训计划：
> - 第1-2周：入职基础
> - 第3-4周：岗位特定技能
> - 第5-8周：跨职能项目
> - 第9-10周：领导力发展
> - 第11-12周：评估和反馈
> 每个模块包括讲座、实践练习和同行评审...

AI的输出结构清晰，听起来很专业。

**问题**：
1. 这个AI交互有什么问题？你会如何重做你的提示？（5分）
2. 得到更好的回应后，你给AI的第二个提示会是什么？（5分）
3. 你怎么知道何时应该停止与AI迭代，开始在现实世界中做些什么？（5分）

**Scoring rubric / 评分标准**:

| Score | Q1: What's Wrong | Q2: Second Prompt | Q3: When to Stop |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| 0-1 | "The prompt was too vague" (correct but shallow) | A refinement of the same approach ("make it more detailed") | "When the plan looks complete" (no real criteria) |
| 2-3 | "The prompt gave no context about our specific needs, culture, or problems. I'd start with our actual challenges" | Asks AI to challenge the plan: "What could go wrong? What assumptions are we making?" | "When I have a testable hypothesis — something I can try with a small group" |
| 4-5 | "The REAL problem: I asked for a solution before defining the problem. The prompt should be: 'Here's what I've observed: [specific problems]. Help me figure out what's actually causing these issues.' The AI gave me a generic plan because I asked a generic question" | Uses AI as a thinking partner: "Interview 5 employees and tell me what patterns you see" or "What would a completely unconventional approach look like — one that has nothing to do with traditional training?" | "The AI's value follows a curve: high at first (frames the problem), then diminishing (polishing details). I'd stop when I catch myself optimizing the PLAN instead of testing the REALITY. The first real-world data point is worth more than 10 more AI iterations" |

## Administration Guide / 实施指南

### Internal Screen / 内部筛选
1. Send the 12 MC questions via any survey tool (Google Forms, Feishu, etc.)
2. Do NOT reveal scoring in advance
3. Set a 15-minute time limit
4. Score automatically using the point values above
5. Share results as dimension profiles, not just total scores

### External Interview / 外部面试
1. **Part 1** (15 min): 12 MC questions, self-administered (paper or screen)
2. **Part 2** (15 min): Read both scenarios, write responses
3. **Scoring**: MC scored automatically. Open-ended scored by 2 independent raters using the rubric above. Average the rater scores.
4. **Calibration**: Before first use, have 3 interviewers independently score the same 5 sample responses. Discuss any scoring discrepancies to align on rubric interpretation.

### Anti-Gaming Notes / 防刷题说明
- Questions are designed so that the "highest scoring" answer is NOT the longest or most complex — it's the one that shows deeper thinking
- D options are sometimes shorter than C options
- The scenarios have planted clues that reward careful reading (e.g., the 40% one-feature data point in Scenario 1)
- Consider creating 2-3 parallel versions with different scenarios for repeat test-takers
